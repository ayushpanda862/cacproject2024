Executive Branch

The Executive Branch is responsible for implementing and enforcing federal laws. It oversees various departments that manage national policies and services.

The President and His Duties

The President leads the nation, enforces laws, and acts as the Commander-in-Chief of the armed forces.

The Vice President and His Duties

The Vice President assists the President and is next in line for the presidency, also presiding over the Senate.

The Cabinet

The Cabinet is composed of the heads of federal departments who advise the President on various policy issues.

Executive Orders

Executive orders allow the President to manage federal operations and direct agencies without congressional approval.

Federal Agencies and Departments

Various federal agencies implement specific policies and regulations, such as the EPA for environmental protection.

Legislative Branch

The Legislative Branch is responsible for making federal laws and is composed of two chambers: the Senate and the House of Representatives. Together, they create legislation that affects the entire nation.

The House of Representatives

The House represents the population, with members serving two-year terms and focusing on legislation that affects constituents directly.

The Senate

The Senate represents states equally, with two Senators from each state serving six-year terms, often focusing on broader national issues.

Legislative Process & Vetoes

This covers how a bill becomes law, including the process of drafting, committee review, and potential presidential veto.

Committees and Their Functions

Committees play a crucial role in reviewing legislation, holding hearings, and conducting investigations to inform policy decisions.

Bicameralism

Bicameralism allows for a thorough review of legislation and representation of diverse interests, though it can lead to gridlock.

Judicial Branch

The Judicial Branch interprets federal laws and administers justice across the United States, ensuring that laws are applied fairly and that individual rights are protected.

Supreme Court

The Supreme Court is the highest court in the land, responsible for interpreting the Constitution and federal law.

Federal Courts

Federal courts handle cases involving federal laws, disputes between states, and other matters under federal jurisdiction.

Judge and Justice Appointment Process

Judges and justices are appointed by the President and confirmed by the Senate, ensuring checks and balances in the judiciary.

Judicial Review

Judicial review allows the courts to assess the constitutionality of laws and executive actions, maintaining the rule of law.

Landmark Supreme Court Cases

Significant Supreme Court cases have shaped civil rights and liberties, influencing American law and society.
