Major Federal Issues

Inflation

Description: Inflation refers to the increase in prices and the decrease in purchasing power of money, impacting the cost of living for individuals and families.

Bipartisan Cooperation

Description: Bipartisan cooperation involves collaboration between the two major political parties in the U.S.—Democrats and Republicans—to pass legislation and address national issues.

Healthcare Affordability

Description: Healthcare affordability pertains to the financial accessibility of medical services and insurance, impacting individuals' ability to receive necessary care.

Drug Addiction

Description: Drug addiction is a chronic disease characterized by compulsive drug seeking and use, despite harmful consequences, affecting individuals, families, and communities.

Federal Budget Deficit

Description: The federal budget deficit occurs when government expenditures exceed its revenues, leading to increased national debt and financial instability.

Illegal Immigration

Description: Illegal immigration refers to the movement of people into a country without legal authorization, raising concerns about border security, legal status, and social services.

Gun Violence

Description: Gun violence encompasses various forms of violence involving firearms, including homicides, suicides, and mass shootings, which pose significant public safety challenges.

Violent Crime

Description: Violent crime includes offenses such as murder, assault, and robbery, which threaten personal safety and community security.

Moral Values

Description: Moral values refer to the principles and standards of behavior that are considered important by individuals and society, impacting social cohesion and governance.

K-12 School Quality

Description: K-12 school quality pertains to the effectiveness of public education systems in providing students with the skills and knowledge necessary for success in life.

Climate Change

Description: Climate change is a long-term alteration of temperature and typical weather patterns in a place, primarily driven by human activities like fossil fuel consumption, leading to environmental impacts.

International Terrorism

Description: International terrorism involves violent acts carried out by groups or individuals against civilian targets across national borders, often motivated by ideological, political, or religious objectives.
