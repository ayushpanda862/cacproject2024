Frequently Asked Questions

What are the eligibility requirements to vote?

To vote in the U.S., you must be a U.S. citizen, at least 18 years old on or before election day, and meet your state's residency requirements.

How do I register to vote?

You can register online, by mail, or in person, depending on your state. It's important to check the specific methods and deadlines in your area.

What are the different voting methods available?

Voting methods vary by state, including in-person voting on election day, early voting, and mail-in voting (absentee voting).

What do I need to bring to the polls?

Requirements vary, but many states require some form of identification. It's best to check your state’s specific ID requirements before voting.

How can I check my voter registration status?

You can check your voter registration status by visiting your state’s election office website or using resources like CanIVote.org.

What should I do if I encounter problems while voting?

If you experience issues while voting, such as being turned away at the polls, you should contact a local election protection hotline or your state’s election office for assistance.

Can I vote if I have a felony conviction?

Voting rights for individuals with felony convictions vary by state. Some states restore voting rights after serving time, while others may have different requirements.

How do mail-in ballots work?

Mail-in ballots allow voters to receive and submit their votes by mail. Procedures for requesting and returning mail-in ballots differ by state, so be sure to check your state's guidelines.

What if I’m unable to vote in person?

If you cannot vote in person, you may have options such as early voting or mail-in voting. Check your state's election office for available alternatives.

What is voter suppression?

Voter suppression refers to tactics used to discourage or prevent specific groups from voting, which can include strict ID laws, limited polling locations, and misinformation about voting.

How can I get involved in the voting process?

You can get involved by educating yourself about the voting process, helping others register, volunteering as a poll worker, or participating in advocacy efforts for fair voting practices.
